# Atlas AI - UK Immigration Guidance Assistant
## Simple Explanation for Non-Technical Users

**What is Atlas AI?**

Atlas AI is a smart computer program that helps people understand if they can get a UK work visa, specifically the "Skilled Worker Visa." Think of it like a very knowledgeable immigration advisor that lives inside a computer.

**Why was it created?**

Getting a UK work visa is complicated. The rules change often, and the official government website has lots of information that can be confusing. Most people can't afford expensive immigration lawyers. Atlas AI was built to help by:

- Reading and understanding all the complex UK immigration rules
- Talking to people in simple, friendly language
- Asking the right questions to understand someone's situation
- Giving clear answers about visa eligibility
- Always pointing people to official government sources for final decisions

**How does it work? (Simple Version)**

Imagine you're having a conversation with a very smart friend who has read every single UK immigration rule. Here's what happens:

1. **You start chatting**: You tell Atlas AI about your job, salary, where you're from, etc.
2. **It listens carefully**: The system picks out important information like your job title, salary, and country.
3. **It checks the rules**: It compares your situation against all the official UK visa requirements.
4. **It gives you an answer**: It tells you if you're likely eligible and explains why.
5. **It explains everything**: It breaks down complex rules into simple terms you can understand.

**The Technology Behind It (Made Simple)**

Think of Atlas AI as having several "brains" that work together:

**Brain 1: The Rule Keeper**
- This part knows all the official UK immigration rules by heart
- It never forgets a rule and always follows them exactly
- It makes the final decision about visa eligibility

**Brain 2: The Conversation Helper**
- This part understands what you're saying in normal language
- It can tell if you're asking about eligibility, documents, or processing times
- It knows how to ask follow-up questions when it needs more information

**Brain 3: The Information Extractor**
- This part picks out important details from your messages
- It identifies things like your job title, salary amount, and country
- It's like a very careful reader who highlights key information

**Brain 4: The Research Assistant**
- This part can quickly find relevant information from official government websites
- It helps provide up-to-date guidance and specific rule references
- It's like having a librarian who knows exactly where to find immigration information

**Brain 5: The Explainer**
- This part takes complex legal information and explains it in simple terms
- It makes sure you understand why you're eligible or not eligible
- It always reminds you that this is guidance, not legal advice

**What makes it special?**

1. **Always follows the rules**: Unlike some chatbots that might "guess" or make things up, Atlas AI only gives answers based on actual UK immigration rules.

2. **Asks smart questions**: If you don't provide enough information, it knows exactly what to ask to make a proper assessment.

3. **Explains its reasoning**: It doesn't just say "yes" or "no" - it explains exactly why, referencing specific rules.

4. **Stays up-to-date**: It can access the latest information from official government sources.

5. **Keeps it simple**: It translates complex legal language into everyday English.

## Complete Technical Details (Simplified)

**Project Structure**

Atlas AI is organized like a well-structured company with different departments:

**1. The Front Office (API Module)**
- This is like the reception desk of the company
- Handles all incoming requests from users
- Manages the website and chat interface
- Routes questions to the right department

**2. The Conversation Manager (Dialogue Manager)**
- This is like a project manager who keeps track of conversations
- Remembers what you've told it in previous messages
- Knows when to ask follow-up questions
- Manages the flow of the conversation (greeting → gathering info → giving answers)

**3. The Language Experts (NLP Pipeline)**
- This team understands human language
- **Preprocessor**: Cleans up your messages (removes typos, fixes grammar)
- **Intent Classifier**: Figures out what you want (are you asking about eligibility, documents, or processing times?)
- **NER Extractor**: Picks out important details like your job title, salary, and country
- **Confidence Handler**: Decides how sure it is about its understanding

**4. The Rule Department (Rule Engine)**
- This is the most important department - it makes all the final decisions
- Has a complete database of all UK immigration rules
- Checks your situation against every relevant rule
- Uses a points system to determine eligibility
- Never guesses - always follows the official rules exactly

**5. The Research Team (RAG System)**
- This team finds the latest information from government websites
- **Document Loader**: Collects information from official UK government sites
- **Embedder**: Organizes this information so it can be found quickly
- **Retriever**: Finds the exact information needed to answer your questions

**6. The Explainer (GPT Layer)**
- This team takes complex legal information and makes it easy to understand
- Translates government language into everyday English
- Always includes disclaimers that this is guidance, not legal advice
- Checks its explanations to make sure they don't contain false information

**How the Rule Engine Works**

The Rule Engine is like a very strict teacher who follows a checklist:

**Mandatory Rules (Must Pass):**
- **SW-001: Sponsorship** - You need a UK employer who will sponsor you
- **SW-002: Job Type** - Your job must be on the UK's approved list
- **SW-003: Salary** - You must earn at least the minimum required amount
- **SW-004: English** - You must prove you can speak English
- **SW-006: Age** - You must be between 18 and 65 years old
- **SW-007: Money** - You must have enough money to support yourself

**Disqualifying Rules (Fail if Met):**
- **SW-008: Criminal Record** - Certain criminal convictions will disqualify you
- **SW-009: Immigration History** - Previous visa violations can cause problems

**Points System**

The system uses a points-based approach:
- You need 70 points total to qualify
- Some things are worth 20 points (like having a job offer)
- Some things are worth 10 points (like having a PhD in a science field)
- Some things are worth 0 points but are still required

**Salary Rules (The Most Complicated Part)**

Salary requirements depend on several factors:

1. **General Threshold**: £38,700 per year (minimum)
2. **Going Rate**: The typical salary for your specific job (varies by occupation)
3. **New Entrant**: If you're young or just starting your career, lower thresholds apply
4. **Shortage Occupation**: If your job is in high demand, you might get special treatment

The system always uses the highest of these amounts as your requirement.

**Job Classification System**

The UK uses a system called SOC (Standard Occupational Classification) to categorize jobs:

- **2136**: Programmers and software development professionals
- **2225**: Nurses
- **2211**: Medical practitioners (doctors)
- **2121**: Civil engineers
- **2421**: Chartered and certified accountants

Each job has a specific "going rate" that determines the salary requirement.

**Machine Learning Components**

Atlas AI uses several AI models, but they're carefully controlled:

**Intent Classification Model**
- Uses a technology called DistilBERT (a smaller, faster version of BERT)
- Trained on thousands of examples of immigration-related questions
- Can identify 4 types of intents:
  1. Eligibility check (am I qualified?)
  2. Document requirements (what do I need?)
  3. Processing times (how long will it take?)
  4. General questions (what is this visa?)

**NER (Named Entity Recognition) Model**
- Uses BERT technology to identify important information in your messages
- Looks for 5 types of information:
  1. Job titles (software engineer, nurse, etc.)
  2. Salaries (£50,000, $60,000, etc.)
  3. Ages (28, 35, etc.)
  4. Countries (India, USA, etc.)
  5. Visa types (Skilled Worker, Health Care, etc.)

**Embedding Model**
- Uses sentence-transformers to understand the meaning of text
- Creates "fingerprints" of documents so similar information can be found quickly
- Powers the search system that finds relevant government guidance
